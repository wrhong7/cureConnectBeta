Detailed Job Descriptions
Whether an individual faces a possible cancer diagnosis, or has been diagnosed with cancer, it is very stressful. Patients understandably have questions and concerns about the disease. The role of the Genetic Counselor is to educate patients about their chance of developing cancers, help them obtain personal meaning from cancer genetic information, and empower them to make educated, informed decisions about genetic testing, cancer screening, and cancer prevention. The Genetic Counselor also helps to support the clinical team’s research protocols related to high-risk cancer patients. You will be the calm and reassuring presence patients need offering pre and post test counseling necessary to collect relevant information needed to assess a patient’s personal and family medical history. As a member of our cancer center team, you'll discover what sets us apart - passion for the individuals and families in our care. Preferred Criteria Bilingual language skills Grant-writing skills and/or other research preparation skills preferred. Required Criteria Graduate degree in Human Genetic-related field. Board certification/eligibility in genetic counseling. Experience in cancer genetic counseling. Experience working in medical settings and interacting collaboratively with medical teams. Demonstrates an ability to function independently in a goal directed manner while communicating effectively with patient, family, significant others and providers at all levels. Demonstrates sensitivity to patient/family belief systems, ethnicity and culture as well as socioeconomic background. Join a hospital where employee engagement is at an all-time high. Enjoy competitive compensation along with benefits such as tuition reimbursement, hospital retirement contributions, and financial planning assistance. Start your life-changing journey today.
